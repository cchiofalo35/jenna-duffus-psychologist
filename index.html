How I Work

Evidence-based, personally tailored

I draw from Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT) and Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT), alongside integrative and parts-informed work. These frameworks inform the structure of our sessions, but the work is always tailored to you.

Nervous System

Your nervous system shapes how you experience the world, in both body and mind. It influences your sense of safety and alertness, and plays a central role in how attention, memory, learning, and emotional responses are organised. We build regulation so your system becomes steadier, creating the conditions for clearer thinking, greater presence, and meaningful change.

Learn more

Core Beliefs

Core beliefs form through experience. Over time, they become the lens through which you interpret yourself, other people, and the world around you. They influence your emotional reactions, your behaviour, your expectations, and the patterns that quietly shape your life. We identify the beliefs organising your internal world and update them so it reflects who you are now, not who you had to be then.

Learn more

Relationship to Self

The relationship you have with yourself shapes how you experience everything else. It influences your emotional tone, your confidence, your boundaries, and the way you move through relationships. When conditional self-regard becomes the default, your internal world can feel tense and effortful. We reshape that relationship so it becomes secure and self-compassionate, creating internal stability rather than tension.

Working Together

What it's like to work together

01

Who This is For

This work is suited to thoughtful, capable people who find themselves facing the same challenges in different forms — in relationships, work, decision-making, or self-worth. You may not fully see the structure beneath it yet, but the pattern feels familiar. You're ready to understand what's driving it and change it at its root.

02

The Experience

Sessions are structured without being rigid. There's direction in the background, but space for what's present on the day. We identify patterns, build insight gradually, and work in a way that allows change to integrate rather than feel forced.

03

What Changes

Over time, clients often describe feeling more settled in themselves. Symptoms become less dominant, reactions less automatic, and decision-making clearer. There is greater self-understanding, more compassion, and a growing sense of internal stability that extends into relationships, work, and everyday life.
